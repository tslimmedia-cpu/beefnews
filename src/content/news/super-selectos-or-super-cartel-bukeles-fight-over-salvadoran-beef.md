El Salvador [eats more beef than it raises](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/el-salvador-import-requirements-and-documentation). Imports already cover over **90% of demand**, with **Nicaragua supplying roughly three-quarters** and the U.S. a distant second at around 5%. For years, that dependency meant higher prices and little consumer choice.

![](https://beefnews.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/image-24.png)

President Nayib Bukele of El Salvador

President **Nayib Bukele** has decided to [break that cartel dynamic](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/el-salvador-begins-transfers-mega-prison-amid-gang-crackdown-2023-02-24/). In **October 2024**, after his trip to Buenos Aires, **Argentina’s Foreign Ministry** [**confirmed**](https://elsalvadorinenglish.com/2024/10/25/argentina-exports-first-shipment-of-chilled-beef-to-el-salvador-opening-new-commercial-opportunities/) **the first refrigerated beef shipment to El Salvador**. And on **July 9, 2025**, **Brazil’s Agriculture Ministry (MAPA) and Foreign Ministry (MRE) jointly announced** that El Salvador formally opened its market to Brazilian beef, [citing](http://agrolink.com.br/noticias/abertura-de-mercado-em-el-salvador-para-carne-bovina_503757.html) more than **$283 million spent on beef imports last year**.

[![](https://beefnews.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/iats-logo-new-sm.jpg)](https://iamtexasslim.org)

Bukele’s rationale is simple: *competition lowers prices and raises quality.* As he said in [Buenos Aires](https://elsalvadorinenglish.com/2024/10/25/argentina-exports-first-shipment-of-chilled-beef-to-el-salvador-opening-new-commercial-opportunities/), Argentine beef would “increase competition, causing prices to fall and quality to improve”.

So far, so good. But the test of reform is never the idea — **it’s who shows up to exploit it.**

###### **The Sanitary Stamp Game**

Every pound of beef that crosses El Salvador’s border passes through a web of sanitary certificates. For U.S. exports, **FSIS export** [**library rules**](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=FAIRS+Export+Certificate+Report+Annual_San+Salvador_El+Salvador_ES2024-0005.pdf) still reflect long-standing bans on beef from cattle over 30 months due to [BSE risk](https://ers.usda.gov/sites/default/files/_laserfiche/outlooks/37390/29425_ldpm14301_002.pdf?v=50781).

![](https://beefnews.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/ad_4nxcrkilzps7pabjyvk7d_bl-nrbniw_s6wmdtbvxge76jresf3ea1kaxo-q6kwkcfcopqyw2zcl2xy0oatsds1xy48rrkoqcqt7e89rznioyzpwee-sbxbrhjeo2ehk-7jtoq0wqpq.png)

“New World screwworm”

For regional players, the barrier is disease. On **June 7, 2025**, **USDA APHIS officially** [**added El Salvador**](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/06/13/2025-10802/addition-of-el-salvador-and-belize-to-the-list-of-regions-affected-with-screwworm#:~:text=Department%20of%20Agriculture-,Animal%20and%20Plant%20Health%20Inspection%20Service,FOR%20FURTHER%20INFORMATION%20CONTACT:) **to the list of regions affected by New World screwworm**, tightening controls and raising questions about animal-health oversight. The Ministry of Agriculture (MAG) has responded with **vaccination and deworming** [**campaigns**](https://dinero.com.sv/en/miscellaneous/mag-strengthens-livestock-sector-health-in-nueva-concepcion-against-screwworm/) in Chalatenango and Santa Ana.

###### **Enter JBS**

Brazil’s new export opening isn’t just about “Brazil.” It’s about **JBS**, the world’s largest meat processor — and its most notorious.

- In **2020**, JBS parent company **J&F Investimentos** [**pleaded guilty**](https://www.justice.gov/usao-edny/pr/jf-investimentos-sa-pleads-guilty-and-agrees-pay-more-256-million-criminal-fines) **in U.S. federal court** to conspiring to violate the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, paying a **$256.5 million criminal penalty**.
- Parallel [**SEC enforcement**](https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2020-254) forced the Batistas to disgorge millions in ill-gotten gains.
- On **June 26, 2025**, JBS completed its long-planned **NYSE listing**, cementing Wall Street backing despite a record that includes [**Operation Car Wash bribes**](https://www.reuters.com/article/world/after-seven-years-brazil-shuts-down-car-wash-anti-corruption-squad-idUSKBN2A407C/)**,** [**environmental fines**](https://apnews.com/article/amazon-deforestation-cattle-jbs-nyse-brazil-76bd1fe0c0f3cd77f0e5a4be8e366ab5)**, and** [**labor scandals**](https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/whd/whd20250113).

**Winners and Losers**

- **Winners:** JBS and other exporters who can ship high-value cuts directly to Salvadoran supermarkets (Walmart, Super Selectos, PriceSmart), which already [dominate](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/el-salvador-agricultural-sectors) **40% of food sales**.\
- **Losers:** Salvadoran ranchers, already squeezed by drought, disease, and decades of decline. The national herd shrank through the civil war years and, even after partial recovery, stood at just **1.59 million head in 2022** according to the [Ministry of Agriculture](https://www.mag.gob.sv/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/1-Anuario-de-Estadi%CC%81sticas-Agropecuarias-2022-2023-final-1.pdf). With imports now covering more than **90% of beef demand**, local producers risk being pushed out of their own market altogether.

The monopoly math is simple: **ranchers lose, consumers depend, monopolists profit.**

###### **The Shield**

![](https://beefnews.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/ad_4nxecqyfhfeja2c8jhyyhgif2ojf-tpq4g63hklj_svspxdyz-oaq4v94ooyxxckqk4h5gsukmkdx_u9hym_dm1vc8l-5phzyrumjfxvjuylvkfesnac9xrkymgkxkvv8n6h7l1cbgw.png)

This is not a story of Bukele selling out. **It’s the opposite.** He opened the door to competition — a logical extension of his [**Bitcoin experiment**](https://www.reutersconnect.com/item/el-salvadors-president-nayib-bukele-present-the-plan-of-bitcoin-city-at-the-closing-on-the-bitcoin-week-in-teoteeque/dGFnOnJldXRlcnMuY29tLDIwMjE6bmV3c21sX1JDMkxZUTlKUFdaUQ) and his anti-gang sovereignty project.

But a free market only works if it’s **free from monopolists.** And JBS is not a competitor — it’s a **cartel in corporate form.**

If Salvadorans want the benefits of Bukele’s reforms — lower prices, better quality, real choice — they’ll need to **guard the door.** Support regenerative ranchers. Demand transparency in imports.
