Amid recent attacks on critical infrastructure and supply chains, a prominent D.C. activist took aim at data centers in a now viral social media video urging white leftists to “continue burning shit down.” The video, which describes the destruction of private property as “non-violent,” has ignited debate over the escalation in radical leftist tactics amid highly coordinated protests and foreign funding.

Arianna “Afeni” Evans, an Army veteran turned paid agitator, took to social media wearing an “Immigrants Forever, ICE Agents Never” hat and she praised recent arson attacks while describing property destruction as “nonviolent” when directed at “things, not people.” She lauded recent warehouse fires as effective resistance. “AI data centers next,” Evans said.

The U.S. has over 3,000 operating data centers and over 1,500 in the development stages, the Midwest region has 655 existing data centers and another 419 planned, The Midwesterner previously reported.

The Midwest states with the most operating and planned data centers are Illinois (262) and Ohio (223).

While Michigan isn’t listed as one of the top-15 states with the highest total number of data centers operating and planned, the state has numerous projects in development across the state, often opposed by communities.

In the video, which has circulated widely on social media after being highlighted by conservative investigative accounts, Evans then urged white leftists to recruit and “organize your people.” “Continue to burn shit down,” Evans said.

Evans, who adopted the name Afeni in homage to Black Panther activist Afeni Shakur, has become increasingly radicalized following the 2020 George Floyd riots. She’s known for agitation tactics, including an August 2025 event where Evans refused to pay a D.C. transit authority fare, then used the optics of her arrest for political mobilization.
