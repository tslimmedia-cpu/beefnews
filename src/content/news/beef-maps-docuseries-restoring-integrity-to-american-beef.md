In collaboration with **[CrowdHealth Carnivore](http://joincrowdhealth.com/carnivore)**, **[The Beef Initiative](http://beefinitiative.com)** proudly presents the **[Beef Maps](http://beefmaps.com) Docuseries**—an immersive look into the lives of ranchers who are bringing integrity and transparency back to American beef. This series doesn’t just tell the story of cattle and land; it’s about people, families, and communities standing up for something that matters.

**[RS "Ruffshot" June](https://beefnews.org/user/rs+june/?profiletab=posts)** and **Texas Slim** embarked on this journey like pioneers of old—reminiscent of the legendary cattle drives led by **Charles Goodnight**. Their trail stretched from **Texas to Kansas**, just like the **[Goodnight-Loving Trail](https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/goodnight-loving-trail)**, where cowboys herded thousands of cattle, navigating through rugged landscapes. It’s a history rooted in grit, and these [#BeefIntelligence](https://www.google.com/search?q=%23beefintelligence&rlz=1C5CHFA_enUS1053US1053&oq=%23beefintelligence&gs_lcrp=EgZjaHJvbWUqBggAEEUYOzIGCAAQRRg7MgoIARAAGIAEGKIEMgYIAhBFGD3SAQgzMjExajBqNKgCALACAA&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8) explorers are modern-day cattlemen continuing the tradition.

> Growing up in the midst of urban and suburban sprawl, I had no idea that just a few miles off every major highway was cattle country. It felt like a whole world had been hidden from my imagination. Beef Maps is about reclaiming our shared agricultural roots and celebrating the unique spirit of America's food producers.
>
> - RS "Ruffshot" june\
> Executive Producer, Beef Maps

But just like the old cattle drives, where independence and decentralized systems ruled, things changed over the years. Consolidation and centralization took over the beef industry, making it harder for small ranchers to thrive. The goal of The Beef Initiative and **[Beef Maps](https://beefmaps.com)** is to reverse that course of history, bringing **decentralization** back to American food and culture. This is more than a series—it’s a movement to reconnect consumers with the source of their food.

As the journey unfolded, June reflected, "We’re no longer just cattlemen or consumers. We’re stewards of the land, preserving a way of life that’s become endangered in the face of corporate dominance.” The wide-open spaces weren’t just a reminder of freedom—they were a call to action: “It’s time we reclaim our food, our health, and our heritage." The docuseries brings that call to life by showcasing ranchers living these principles every day.
